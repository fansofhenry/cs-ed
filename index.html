Research Portfolio

I study why students leave STEM — and what computational tools can do about it.

I'm Henry Fan, a McNair Scholar at San José State University and Research Lead at the Foothill College Science Learning Institute. My research sits at the intersection of CS education, learning analytics, and human-centered computing.

The connecting thread is structural equity: the idea, grounded in Seymour and Hunter's landmark work, that students leave STEM not because they can't do the work but because the structures around them weren't designed with them in mind. I'm building toward a research program that makes those structures visible and changeable — through interview studies, NLP analysis of course materials, and computational tools for instructional auditing.

Before this research, I spent years working across student services at Foothill College — financial aid, counseling, tutoring, learning communities — watching the same students encounter the same invisible structural barriers from every vantage point the institution offers. That experience is the origin of this work.

I'm applying to PhD programs in CS education and learning sciences for Fall 2026.
